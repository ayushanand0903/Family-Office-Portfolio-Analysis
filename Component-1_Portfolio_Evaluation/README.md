## Project Overview

This project focused on evaluating the performance of an existing Family Office equity portfolio through a combination of Fundamental Analysis and Technical Analysis.

The objective was to assess the financial health, market performance, and future prospects of each stock in the portfolio and recommend which holdings should be retained or exited based on data-driven investment analysis.

---

## Business Objective

Family Offices require periodic portfolio reviews to ensure capital is allocated to companies with strong fundamentals, sustainable growth potential, and acceptable risk levels.

This analysis aimed to:

* Evaluate the performance of existing portfolio holdings
* Identify financially strong and weak companies
* Assess stock price momentum and market sentiment
* Recommend portfolio actions (Hold / Monitor / Exit)
* Improve long-term portfolio quality

## Methodology

### Fundamental Analysis

A three-year financial analysis was conducted for each company using the following metrics:

#### Profitability Analysis

* Earnings Per Share (EPS)
* Profitability Ratios
* Return on Investment (ROI)

#### Valuation Analysis

* Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E)
* Price-to-Sales Ratio (P/S)

#### Financial Health Assessment

* Liquidity Ratios
* Solvency Ratios
* Degree of Leverage

#### Growth Analysis

* Revenue Trends
* Earnings Trends
* Business Growth Performance

Each company was evaluated individually and conclusions were drawn based on overall financial strength and growth sustainability.

---

### Technical Analysis

Technical indicators were used to evaluate market momentum and identify potential investment actions.

#### Indicators Used

* Trendline Analysis
* Relative Strength Index (RSI)

The analysis helped identify overbought and oversold conditions and supported investment decisions alongside the fundamental assessment.

---

## Key Findings

### Safe Long-Term Investments

#### Hindustan Unilever (HUL)

* Strong financial stability
* Consistent earnings performance
* Defensive business model
* Suitable for long-term wealth preservation

#### Bharat Electronics (BEL)

* Strong growth potential
* Beneficiary of defence sector expansion
* Healthy financial position
* Attractive long-term investment opportunity

---

### Moderate Risk, Stable Return Investments

#### ONGC

* Stable cash-generating business
* Exposure to commodity price fluctuations
* Suitable for monitoring and selective allocation

#### NTPC

* Consistent earnings profile
* Strong market position in the power sector
* Moderate risk with steady return potential

---

### High-Risk Investment

#### Tata Steel

* Cyclical industry exposure
* Greater earnings volatility
* Higher uncertainty regarding future performance
* Considered the riskiest stock within the portfolio

---

## Technical Analysis Insights

### RSI-Based Observations

* Monitor ONGC and HUL for potential reversal signals.
* Hold BEL and NTPC while tracking market conditions.
* Consider profit booking in Tata Steel to protect gains and reduce portfolio risk.

## Final Conclusion

The analysis identified HUL and BEL as the strongest long-term holdings due to their financial strength and growth prospects.

NTPC and ONGC demonstrated stable fundamentals but require ongoing monitoring because of sector-specific risks.

Tata Steel exhibited the highest risk profile due to cyclical industry dynamics and earnings volatility, making it the primary candidate for portfolio reduction or profit booking.

The combination of Fundamental Analysis and Technical Analysis enabled a structured, data-driven evaluation of the portfolio and supported informed investment decision-making.
