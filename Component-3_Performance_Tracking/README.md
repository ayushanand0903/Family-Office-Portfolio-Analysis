## Project Overview

After constructing a diversified portfolio of fundamentally strong companies, the next phase involved monitoring stock performance and evaluating the effectiveness of the investment strategy.

A simulated investment environment was created using a virtual capital allocation of ₹10,00,000. The portfolio was tracked over a two-week observation period to assess stock performance, portfolio returns, and overall investment outcomes.

---

## Objective

The objective of this phase was to:

* Monitor the performance of the newly constructed portfolio
* Evaluate the effectiveness of stock selection decisions
* Measure portfolio returns over the observation period
* Compare actual outcomes against investment expectations
* Validate the proposed investment strategy

## Investment Strategy

The portfolio was designed based on:

* Strong company fundamentals
* Sector diversification
* Long-term growth opportunities
* Risk management principles
* Balanced capital allocation

A virtual investment capital of ₹10,00,000 was distributed across the selected stocks.

---

## Performance Monitoring

The portfolio was monitored continuously over a two-week period.

Key parameters tracked included:

* Individual stock performance
* Portfolio value changes
* Percentage return generated
* Sector contribution to portfolio growth
* Overall portfolio profitability

### Outcome

The newly constructed portfolio appreciated by approximately **2.6% during the observation period**, indicating that the stock selection and allocation strategy generated positive short-term results.

---

## Key Observations

### Diversification Benefits

The portfolio's sector diversification helped reduce concentration risk and contributed to stable overall performance.

### Quality Stock Selection

The selected companies demonstrated resilience and supported portfolio growth during the observation period.

### Risk Management

The combination of defensive sectors (FMCG, Healthcare, Financial Services) and growth sectors (Technology, Infrastructure, Telecom) provided balance and stability.

---

## Conclusion

The portfolio construction strategy successfully generated positive returns during the monitoring period.

The project demonstrated the complete investment management cycle:

1. Analysis of an existing portfolio
2. Identification of strengths and weaknesses
3. Selection of new investment opportunities
4. Portfolio construction and capital allocation
5. Performance tracking and evaluation

The observed portfolio growth of approximately 2.6% validated the effectiveness of the investment framework and highlighted the importance of combining fundamental analysis, technical analysis, diversification, and disciplined portfolio management.
